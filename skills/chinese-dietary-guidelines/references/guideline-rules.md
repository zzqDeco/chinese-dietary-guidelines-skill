# Guideline Rules

Use these rules as the skill's compact knowledge base. They summarize verified local extraction from《中国居民膳食指南（2022）》and preserve PDF page sources for citation.

## Profile Selection

- `adult`: default for healthy adults, PDF 003, 009-011, 153.
- `pregnant_early`, `pregnant_mid`, `pregnant_late`: pregnancy rules, PDF 192, 197.
- `lactating`: lactation rules, PDF 200, 202-204.
- `preschool_2_3`, `preschool_4_5`: children aged 2-5, PDF 260-261.
- `school_age`: children and adolescents aged 6-17, PDF 266-267.
- `older_adult`, `very_old`: 65+ and 80+ older adults, PDF 277-284.
- `vegan`, `lacto_ovo_vegetarian`: adult vegetarian rules, PDF 294-299.

## Adult Targets

Sources: PDF 003, 009-011, 018, 042, 062, 090, 109, 130, 153.

| Item | Target |
|---|---:|
| Food variety | >=12 food types/day and >=25/week |
| Grains | 200-300 g/day |
| Whole grains and legumes | 50-150 g/day |
| Tubers | 50-100 g/day |
| Vegetables | 300-500 g/day |
| Dark vegetables | about half of vegetables |
| Fruits | 200-350 g/day |
| Animal foods | 120-200 g/day |
| Fish | 300-500 g/week, preferably at least twice weekly |
| Eggs | about 1 egg/day; do not discard yolk |
| Dairy | 300-500 g/day |
| Soybeans and nuts | 25-35 g/day |
| Cooking oil | 25-30 g/day |
| Salt | <5 g/day |
| Added sugar | <50 g/day, ideally <25 g/day |
| Alcohol | <=15 g/day if drinking; avoid for children, pregnant/lactating users, and chronic disease risk |
| Drinking water | male 1700 ml/day, female 1500 ml/day at low activity |
| Activity | >=150 min/week moderate activity; about 6000 active steps/day |

## School-Age Children

Sources: PDF 266-267.

- Eat a good breakfast containing at least 3 of these categories: grains/tubers, vegetables/fruits, dairy, animal foods, soy/nuts.
- Drink about 300 g milk or equivalent dairy daily.
- Drink enough water and avoid sugar-sweetened beverages.
- Do not drink alcohol.
- Accumulate at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity daily; include vigorous and muscle/bone strengthening activity at least 3 days/week.
- Limit screen time to 2 hours/day.

## Older Adults

Sources: PDF 277-284.

- 65-79 years: rich food variety, adequate animal foods, frequent soy products, shared meals, outdoor activity, regular nutrition assessment.
- Animal foods: 120-150 g/day, roughly fish 40-50 g, livestock/poultry 40-50 g, eggs 40-50 g.
- Dairy: 300-400 ml milk or equivalent dairy protein daily.
- Soybeans: about 15 g/day.
- Protein: generally 1.0-1.2 g/kg body weight; with resistance training may be >1.2-1.5 g/kg.
- Suitable BMI for older adults: about 20.0-26.9 kg/m².
- 80+ years: choose soft, energy- and nutrient-dense foods; watch weight loss and intake below 80% of target; consult clinicians for oral nutrition supplements.
