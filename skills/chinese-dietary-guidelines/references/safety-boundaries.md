# Safety Boundaries

This skill gives guideline-level dietary planning support. It must not diagnose, treat, or prescribe medical nutrition therapy.

## Escalate or Add Caution

Add a clear caution and recommend clinician or registered dietitian review when the user mentions:

- diabetes, kidney disease, liver disease, gout, cardiovascular disease, hypertension, eating disorders, cancer, digestive disorders, food allergy, or medication-diet conflicts
- pregnancy complications, gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, severe vomiting, anemia, fetal growth concerns
- infant feeding problems, child growth faltering, obesity treatment, failure to thrive
- older adult frailty, swallowing difficulty, unintended weight loss, intake below 80% of target
- rapid weight loss, fasting, extreme calorie restriction, supplements, or nutrient deficiency symptoms

## Recommendation Limits

- Do not prescribe calories or macronutrient targets beyond guideline ranges unless the user supplies a clinician plan.
- Do not recommend alcohol; only flag upper limits where applicable.
- Do not recommend weight loss during pregnancy or for children.
- Avoid supplement advice beyond guideline-level reminders such as folic acid, vitamin D, iodine, or B12 risk; refer to clinicians for dose decisions.
- For allergy or avoid-food entries, exclude those foods from example recommendations and suggest alternatives in the same food group.
