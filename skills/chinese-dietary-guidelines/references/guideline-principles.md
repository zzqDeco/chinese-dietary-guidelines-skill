# Guideline Principles

Use this file as a compact reference for agent reasoning. It summarizes verified local extraction from《中国居民膳食指南（2022）》and preserves PDF page sources. Treat the quantities as anchors for judgment, not as an automatic scoring system.

## General Adult Principles

Sources: PDF 003, 009-011, 018, 042, 062, 090, 109, 130, 153.

| Topic | Principle |
|---|---|
| Food variety | Aim for at least 12 food types per day and 25 per week. |
| Staple foods | Grains 200-300 g/day; include whole grains and mixed legumes 50-150 g/day; tubers 50-100 g/day. |
| Vegetables | 300-500 g/day; dark vegetables should be about half. |
| Fruit | Fresh fruit 200-350 g/day; do not replace fruit with juice. |
| Animal foods | Average 120-200 g/day; prefer fish and poultry; fish 300-500 g/week, preferably at least twice weekly. |
| Eggs | About one egg/day for adults; do not discard the yolk without reason. |
| Dairy | 300-500 g/day milk or equivalent dairy. |
| Soy and nuts | 25-35 g/day soybeans, soy products, and nuts as a reference anchor. |
| Oil and salt | Cooking oil 25-30 g/day as an upper-bound style anchor; salt <5 g/day. |
| Added sugar | <50 g/day, ideally <25 g/day. |
| Alcohol | If drinking, alcohol <=15 g/day; do not recommend alcohol. |
| Water | Low-activity adults: male about 1700 ml/day, female about 1500 ml/day. |
| Activity | At least 150 min/week moderate activity; about 6000 active steps/day. |

## Pregnancy

Sources: PDF 192, 197.

- Do not frame recommendations as weight loss.
- Emphasize appropriate pregnancy weight gain, iron-rich foods, iodized salt, folic acid and vitamin D reminders, outdoor activity, no smoking or alcohol, and breastfeeding preparation.

## Lactation

Sources: PDF 200, 202-204.

- Keep foods diverse but not excessive.
- Increase high-quality protein, vitamin A-rich animal foods, seafood, iodized salt, water, and soups.
- Avoid tobacco and alcohol; limit strong tea and coffee.

| Item | Principle |
|---|---|
| Grains | 225-275 g/day; at least one-third whole grains and legumes. |
| Tubers | About 75 g/day. |
| Vegetables | 400-500 g/day; colored or leafy vegetables at least two-thirds. |
| Fruit | 200-350 g/day. |
| Fish/poultry/eggs/meat | 175-225 g/day. |
| Dairy | 300-500 ml/day. |
| Soybeans and nuts | Soybeans about 25 g/day; nuts about 10 g/day. |
| Oil and salt | Cooking oil about 25 g/day; salt <5 g/day. |
| Water | About 2100 ml/day. |
| Liver | 1-2 times/week, about 85 g pork liver or 40 g chicken liver total. |

## School-Age Children

Sources: PDF 266-267.

- Breakfast should include at least 3 categories among grains/tubers, vegetables/fruits, dairy, animal foods, soy/nuts.
- Drink about 300 g milk or equivalent dairy daily.
- Drink enough water; avoid sugar-sweetened beverages.
- Do not drink alcohol.
- Accumulate at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity daily; include vigorous and muscle/bone strengthening activity at least 3 days/week.
- Limit screen time to 2 hours/day.

## Older Adults

Sources: PDF 277-284.

- Keep food variety high, animal foods adequate, soy products frequent, and meals socially enjoyable where possible.
- 65-79 years: animal foods about 120-150 g/day, roughly fish 40-50 g, livestock/poultry 40-50 g, eggs 40-50 g.
- Dairy: 300-400 ml milk or equivalent dairy protein daily.
- Soybeans: about 15 g/day as an anchor.
- Protein: generally 1.0-1.2 g/kg body weight; with resistance training may be >1.2-1.5 g/kg.
- Suitable BMI for older adults is about 20.0-26.9 kg/m².
- 80+ years: choose soft, energy- and nutrient-dense foods; watch weight loss and intake below 80% of target; refer to clinicians for oral nutrition supplement decisions.
