# Eating Out

Use this file when the user cannot cook or asks what to choose in a cafeteria, delivery app, restaurant, convenience store, travel setting, or social meal.

## Core Principles

- Recommend concrete combinations, not only principles.
- Keep oil, salt, sugar, and sauce uncertainty explicit.
- Prefer steamed, boiled, braised, clear-soup, lightly stir-fried, and sauce-separated choices.
- Add vegetables first when the meal is mostly staple plus meat.
- Use dairy, fruit, soy products, or nuts to fill gaps when a full balanced meal is not available.
- Do not claim precise nutrition for restaurant foods without reliable data.

## Cafeteria

Useful patterns:

| Situation | Recommended Combination |
|---|---|
| Standard lunch | 杂粮饭 or 米饭半份 + 清蒸/炖/少油荤菜 + 两份蔬菜 + 豆腐/蛋类 |
| Vegetable shortage | Add one dark vegetable dish and one lighter vegetable dish. |
| Protein shortage | Choose fish, chicken, egg, tofu, or lean meat; avoid making processed meat the default. |
| High oil/salt risk | Choose dishes with less visible oil, skip most gravy, avoid salty soup base. |

## Delivery

Useful patterns:

| Situation | Recommended Combination |
|---|---|
| Rice bowl | Choose lean protein + two vegetable toppings; ask for sauce separately. |
| Noodles | Add egg/tofu/lean meat and vegetables; avoid drinking salty soup. |
| Light meal salad | Ensure enough staple or tuber if it replaces a main meal; avoid high-sugar dressing. |
| Hot pot or spicy pot | Choose more vegetables, tofu, mushrooms, lean protein; use less dipping sauce and soup. |

## Convenience Store

Useful combinations:

- plain milk or unsweetened yogurt + boiled egg + whole-grain bread + fruit
- rice roll or simple rice meal + salad/vegetable side + unsweetened drink
- soy milk without added sugar + boiled corn or sweet potato + tea egg

Flag uncertainty for sodium in packaged foods and sauces.

## Restaurant Or Social Meal

Useful strategies:

- choose one steamed fish or lean protein dish
- add at least one dark vegetable and one other vegetable dish
- keep fried dishes and heavy sauces as shared small portions
- drink water or unsweetened tea
- avoid recommending alcohol; if alcohol is discussed, use guideline upper-limit language only

## What To Log Afterwards

Ask for:

- restaurant type or dish names
- approximate portion eaten
- visible oil/sauce level
- drinks, alcohol, dessert
- whether vegetables, fruit, dairy, or soy were included

If portion details are vague, log with `confidence=low` and explain the uncertainty.
